Data

Used datasets:

  • World Bank Population Totals- Total popluation in the world for the years 2006-2016.
  • Bank CO2 Emissions by Country- CO2 Emissions by country for years 2006-2016.
  • PyTrends Library- Python library with an API from Google Trends. Used to search the number of times relatable words to climate change were search on Google throughout the years.
  • GeoJSON file- Used for map and country outlines, passed into Leaflet for map visualization.

    • Discussion

    Our visualizations show that there is a strong relationship between income level and total CO2 emissions. We were most surprised to find that some of the smallest countries were the highest CO2 emission producers per capita. This may be explained by the industries that are dominant in those countries, such as oil production and factory work. It is also important to note that data was not available for all countries, and that these visualizations cover a brief period of time. Climate change patterns can be better analyzed and understood over longer periods of times.

    It is also important to note that the Google search trends data was only available using English terms. It is very possible that the overall popularity of search terms related to the environment or climate change are more popular than our data shows in other languages and in other countries.
